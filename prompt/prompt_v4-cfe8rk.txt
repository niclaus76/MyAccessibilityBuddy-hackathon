# EXAMPLES
These are examples of the alt_text field, the "" are used here to delimit text, they must not be included in the alt_text and alt_text_value

- Inclusive language test (disability)
✅ Good: "A person using a wheelchair is entering a building."
❌ Bad: "A brave disabled person is trying to enter a building."

- Inclusive language test (age)
✅ Good: "An older person is reading a book in a park."
❌ Bad: "An old man is struggling to read a book in the park."

- Inclusive language test (gender-neutral)
✅ Good: "Two people are shaking hands."
❌ Bad: "Two businessmen are shaking hands."

- Inclusive language test (disability – vision)
✅ Good: "A person with a visual impairment is crossing the street using a white cane."
❌ Bad: "A blind person is struggling to cross the street."

- Inclusive language test (ethnicity)
✅ Good: "A group of colleagues are sitting around a table."
❌ Bad: "A group of Asian workers are sitting around a table."

- Inclusive language test (role vs. appearance)
✅ Good: "A speaker is addressing an audience at a conference."
❌ Bad: "A woman in a suit is talking at a conference."

- Decorative
✅ Good: ""
❌ Bad: "Beautiful decorative border with floral patterns"

- Informative - Photo
✅ Good: "European Central Bank headquarters in Frankfurt."
❌ Bad: "Picture of the ECB building"
❌ Bad: "The magnificent ECB headquarters towering majestically"

- Functional
✅ Good: "Search"
❌ Bad: "Magnifying glass icon"

- Logo
✅ Good: "European Central Bank logo"
❌ Bad: "Stylized ECB letters in blue with yellow stars"

- Text image
✅ Good: "Emergency Exit"
❌ Bad: "Sign showing text"

- People with context
✅ Good: "ECB President Christine Lagarde speaking at press conference."
❌ Bad: "Woman in business attire talking"

- People without context
✅ Good: "Two people in discussion."
❌ Bad: "Two businesspeople having a meeting"
